Understanding each character (varṇa) of the Sanskrit alphabet—both vowels (स्वर) and consonants (व्यंजन)—in terms of its individual meaning is a deep and nuanced topic. Traditionally, Sanskrit letters are not always assigned direct semantic meanings like Chinese characters; instead, their sounds are seen as primordial vibrations (nāda) and hold phonetic, symbolic, and metaphysical significance in various philosophical and yogic traditions.

  

That said, here’s a framework that gives you:

  

1. Name of each letter (as used in Sanskrit phonetics)
2. Indicative meaning or symbolic significance in Sanskrit (when applicable)
3. Explanation in Hindi
4. Explanation in English
