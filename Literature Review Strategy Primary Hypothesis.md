# Literature Review Strategy: “The Divine Presence: Footprints of God”

## **Phase I: Literature Review Framework Development**

### **A. Search Strategy Architecture**

**Primary Research Questions for Literature Review:**

- What methodologies have been used to document divine interventions historically?
- How have different religious traditions defined and categorized divine manifestations?
- What verification protocols exist for supernatural/miraculous claims?
- What patterns emerge from cross-cultural studies of divine intervention?
- How has academic scholarship approached the study of religious phenomena?
- What technological and scientific methods have been applied to miracle investigation?

**Database and Source Categories:**

- Academic databases and peer-reviewed journals
- Religious institutional archives and publications
- Historical chronicles and biographical accounts
- Medical journals documenting unexplained healings
- Anthropological studies of religious phenomena
- Psychological research on religious experiences

## **Phase IV: Literature Analysis Framework**

### **A. Categorization and Coding System**

**Thematic Classification:**

- **Phenomenological:** Description and characteristics of divine interventions
- **Methodological:** Approaches to documenting and verifying claims
- **Theological:** Religious interpretations and doctrinal frameworks
- **Historical:** Temporal and cultural context analysis
- **Scientific:** Empirical investigations and medical documentation
- **Comparative:** Cross-cultural and inter-religious studies

**Evidence Quality Assessment:**

- **Primary Sources:** Direct witness accounts, contemporary documentation
- **Secondary Sources:** Historical analysis, scholarly interpretation
- **Tertiary Sources:** Compilations, encyclopedias, popular treatments
- **Verification Level:** Multiple sources, single source, unverified claims

**Geographic and Cultural Coding:**

- Regional origin and cultural context
- Religious tradition classification
- Historical period identification
- Language and translation considerations
- Cross-cultural comparative elements

### **B. Critical Analysis Criteria**

**Source Credibility Assessment:**

- Author credentials and institutional affiliation
- Peer review status and academic rigor
- Methodological transparency and reproducibility
- Bias identification and acknowledgment
- Primary source access and citation quality

**Content Evaluation Framework:**

- Definitional clarity and operational criteria
- Evidence presentation and verification methods
- Logical consistency and argument structure
- Cultural sensitivity and interpretive frameworks
- Contribution to field knowledge and methodology
