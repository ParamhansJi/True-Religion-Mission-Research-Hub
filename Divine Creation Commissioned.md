## Scientific Method 

Response.
The phrase “systematic study of observable phenomena via experiment” refers to the scientific method — the foundational approach used in science to understand the natural world. Let’s break it down:

---

🔍 Key Components Explained

• Systematic Study
This means the investigation follows a structured, organized, and repeatable process. It avoids random guessing and relies on logic and consistency.
• Observable Phenomena
These are events or behaviors that can be seen, measured, or detected — anything from the motion of planets to chemical reactions or human behavior.
• Via Experiment
Experiments are controlled procedures designed to test hypotheses. They allow scientists to manipulate variables and observe outcomes to draw conclusions.


---

🧪 What It Describes: The Scientific Method

Step	Description	
1. Observation	Noticing and describing a phenomenon or pattern.	
2. Question	Asking why or how something happens.	
3. Hypothesis	Proposing a testable explanation.	
4. Experiment	Designing and conducting tests to gather data.	
5. Analysis	Interpreting results to see if they support the hypothesis.	
6. Conclusion	Drawing insights and possibly refining the hypothesis.	
7. Replication	Repeating the experiment to verify results.	


---

📘 Example

Let’s say you observe that plants grow faster in sunlight than in shade.

• You form a hypothesis: “Sunlight increases plant growth.”
• You design an experiment: Grow two sets of plants, one in sunlight, one in shade.
• You measure growth over time.
• You analyze the data and conclude whether sunlight truly affects growth.
